The Source Code

This project operates at the intersection of data science and investigative journalism. Because Conflict-Related Sexual Violence (CRSV) is an inherently silenced crime, "raw data" is never enough. It represents the floor, not the ceiling, of reality.

Below, we detail our data sources, the algorithmic logic used to project hidden figures, and the ethical frameworks applied to protect dignity while exposing truth.

I. Primary Datasets

The quantitative backbone of this project relies on two publicly available, verified datasets.

1. ACLED Conflict Index (2024)

Source: Armed Conflict Location & Event Data Project

Usage: Used to calculate the "Danger Score" and "Deadliness" metrics for the suppression algorithm. Provides the geospatial context for conflict intensity.

2. HDX Humanitarian Data Exchange

Source: OCHA / Humanitarian Data Exchange

Usage: Provided the baseline "Verified Incidents" (The Gold Dots). This dataset includes geolocated reports of sexual violence verified by aid workers and medical staff.

II. The "Roots" Algorithm

To move beyond verified reports and visualize the "Dark Figure" of unreported crime, we developed a Saturation Model based on the following logic:

The Formula

Suppression = (Danger × 0.7) + (Deadliness × 0.3)

We applied a Saturation Cap. Once a region's Danger Score exceeds 2,000 (e.g., Sudan, Gaza), the suppression multiplier hits its ceiling (approx. 2000x). This prevents statistical outliers from skewing the scale while acknowledging that in total war, reporting drops to near zero.

The Peace Protocol: Countries classified as "Low/Inactive" by ACLED were forced to a 1x multiplier to prevent false projections in non-conflict zones.

IV. Ethical Statement

Visualizing sexual violence requires a balance between Exposure and Dignity. We adhered to the following principles:

  • No Individual Identifiers: All geospatial data points (The Gold Dots) were aggregated or randomized within a 5km radius to protect the privacy of survivors.
  • Imputed Demographics: The age distributions shown in Chapter 3 and 4 are probabilistic models based on the cited reports. They represent statistical patterns, not specific individual case files.
  • The "Dark Figure" Disclaimer: All "Projected" numbers are estimates based on conflict intensity models. They should be interpreted as an indication of scale, not a census.
